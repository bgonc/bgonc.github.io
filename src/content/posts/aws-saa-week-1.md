# AWS SAA week 1

I started my AWS Solutions Architect Associate study path this week. My focus was on core services and getting hands-on time instead of only reading docs.

## IAM fundamentals

I spent most of the first sessions on IAM users, groups, roles, and policies. The goal was to understand permission boundaries properly before touching bigger architectures.

## EC2 and VPC basics

I reviewed EC2 instance types and pricing models, then practiced launching instances inside a simple VPC layout with public and private subnets.

I also reviewed security groups and NACL differences since that can be confusing early on.

## S3 and storage choices

I compared common S3 storage classes and practiced lifecycle rules. I wanted to understand where cost and access pattern decisions actually matter.

## Week 1 takeaway

The biggest lesson so far is that AWS design questions are mostly tradeoff questions. Cost, availability, and operational complexity are always connected.

Next week I will focus on load balancing, autoscaling, and high availability patterns.
